About this VAT calculator

VAT (Value Added Tax) is a consumption tax added to most goods and services across Europe and many other regions. This calculator works both directions: add VAT when you have a net (tax-exclusive) price, or remove it to find the net hidden inside a gross (tax-inclusive) price — handy for invoices, expenses and bookkeeping.

The maths

  • Adding VAT: gross = net × (1 + rate/100). The VAT is gross − net.
  • Removing VAT: net = gross ÷ (1 + rate/100). This is "reverse VAT" — you can't just subtract the percentage, because the percentage was applied to the smaller net figure.

Common EU rates include 20% (France, UK historically), 21% (Spain, Netherlands, Belgium), 19% (Germany), 22% (Italy) and 23% (Ireland, Portugal). Always check your local standard and reduced rates.

FAQ

Why can't I just subtract 21% to remove VAT?

Because the 21% was added to the net amount, not the gross. Removing it requires dividing by 1.21, which gives a slightly different (correct) figure.

Does it work for US sales tax?

Yes — sales tax adds the same way. Enter your local rate in the rate field.
