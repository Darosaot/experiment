About compound interest

Compound interest is interest earned on your interest. Over long periods it produces dramatic, accelerating growth — which is why starting early matters so much. This calculator combines a starting balance with regular monthly contributions and your chosen compounding frequency.

Tips

  • Time is the biggest lever. An extra decade of compounding often beats a much larger contribution.
  • Contribute regularly. Small monthly amounts add up enormously over decades.
  • Rate matters, but so does consistency. Don't chase returns at the cost of staying invested.

This is an educational tool, not financial advice. Real investments carry risk and returns vary year to year.

FAQ

Are contributions added before or after interest?

Contributions are added each month and then earn interest in subsequent periods, which is the standard convention for regular-savings projections.

Does it account for inflation or tax?

No — results are nominal (before inflation and tax). For "real" purchasing power, subtract your expected inflation rate from the interest rate.
