How to calculate percentages

A percentage is just a fraction of 100. These three calculators cover the questions people ask most:

  • X% of Y = (X ÷ 100) × Y. Example: 15% of 200 = 30. Great for tips, discounts and taxes.
  • X is what % of Y = (X ÷ Y) × 100. Example: 30 is 25% of 120. Useful for test scores and progress.
  • Percentage change = ((Y − X) ÷ X) × 100. A positive result is an increase, a negative one a decrease.

FAQ

How do I calculate a discount?

Use "X% of Y" with the discount percent and the original price to get the amount off, then subtract it from the price.

What's the difference between percentage points and percent?

Going from 10% to 15% is a 5 percentage-point rise, but a 50% increase relative to the starting value. The change calculator above gives the relative percent.
