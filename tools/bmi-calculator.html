About Body Mass Index

BMI is a simple screening number that relates your weight to your height. It's calculated as weight in kilograms divided by height in metres squared (kg/m²). It's widely used because it's quick and needs no equipment, though it doesn't distinguish muscle from fat.

The standard categories (adults)

  • Under 18.5 — Underweight
  • 18.5 – 24.9 — Healthy weight
  • 25.0 – 29.9 — Overweight
  • 30.0 and above — Obese

BMI is a general guide, not a diagnosis. Athletes and very muscular people may have a high BMI without excess fat. Always consult a doctor for personal health advice.

FAQ

Is BMI accurate for everyone?

It's a useful population-level screen but less accurate for athletes, the elderly, and during pregnancy. Treat it as one data point among many.

Does it work for children?

Children and teens use age- and sex-specific BMI percentiles instead of the adult categories above.
