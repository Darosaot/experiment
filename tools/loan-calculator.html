How the loan calculator works

This calculator uses the standard amortizing-loan formula used by banks for mortgages, car loans and personal loans. You pay the same amount every month; early payments are mostly interest, later payments are mostly principal. The formula is:

M = P · r · (1+r)ⁿ / ((1+r)ⁿ − 1)

where P is the principal, r is the monthly interest rate (annual rate ÷ 12), and n is the number of monthly payments (years × 12).

Tips to pay less interest

  • Shorten the term. A shorter term means higher monthly payments but far less total interest.
  • Shop the rate. Even a 0.5% lower rate can save thousands over a mortgage's life.
  • Make extra payments. Any amount above the minimum goes straight to principal and shortens the loan.

FAQ

Does this include taxes and insurance?

No. It calculates principal and interest only (P&I). For a mortgage, add property tax, homeowners insurance and any HOA fees separately.

What currency does it use?

The calculator is currency-agnostic — enter the numbers in euros, dollars, pounds or any currency and the results are in the same unit.

Is my financial data private?

Completely. All math happens in your browser. Nothing you type is sent anywhere.
