# Pricing & the honest €150/month math

## The target, in plain numbers
You want **≥ €150/month**. Here's what that takes at different prices, after
typical platform fees.

| Price | Platform | Fees (approx) | You keep / sale | Sales/mo for €150 |
|-------|----------|---------------|-----------------|-------------------|
| €12   | Gumroad  | 10% flat      | ~€10.80         | **~14 sales**     |
| €12   | Etsy     | ~6.5% + €0.30 listing/txn + ~4% payment | ~€10.40 | **~15 sales** |
| €19   | Gumroad  | 10%           | ~€17.10         | **~9 sales**      |
| €19   | Etsy     | ~10–11% all-in| ~€16.90         | **~9 sales**      |

**Takeaway:** ~9–15 sales a month gets you there. That's roughly one sale every
2–3 days. Very achievable for a useful template *if the listing gets seen* —
which is why traffic (see `promotion-free-traffic.md`) matters more than price.

## Recommended starting price
**€12.** Low enough to be an impulse buy, high enough that ~14 sales/month
clears your goal. Once you have a few reviews, test **€16–19** — conversion
rarely drops much and your per-sale margin jumps, lowering the sales you need.

## Pricing tactics that work for templates
- **Anchor with a "regular price."** List at €12, occasionally run a "€19 → €12"
  sale. Etsy/Gumroad both show the strikethrough; it lifts conversion.
- **Bundle later.** Once this sells, make a 2nd template (e.g. a content
  calendar) and sell the pair for €19. Bundles raise average order value with
  no new traffic.
- **Don't go below €9.** Ultra-cheap signals low quality and you'll need 2x the
  sales for the same income.

## Which platform first?
**Etsy.** It has buyers actively searching "freelancer spreadsheet" right now;
Gumroad sends you no traffic on its own. List on Etsy for organic discovery,
add Gumroad as a zero-fee-to-list second channel and as the link you drop in
social posts / your profile.

## Realistic timeline (no paid ads)
- **Weeks 1–4:** listing indexes on Etsy, first 1–3 sales, first reviews.
- **Months 2–3:** reviews + Etsy "quality score" compound; sales climb toward
  the ~10–15/month range if the niche keywords are right.
- This is *not* instant. The asset is built once; the ramp depends on reviews
  and search ranking, which take a few weeks. Be patient and don't delist.

## The one lever that beats everything
**Reviews.** Etsy ranking is heavily review-driven. After each sale, the
quick-start guide already invites buyers to reach out — also send a short,
polite "thanks, a review really helps a tiny shop like mine 🙏" message. Five
reviews changes everything.
