# Getting to €150/month — the free-traffic playbook

The product is built. Income now depends on **eyeballs on the listing**. Here
is the lowest-effort path to enough traffic, honestly ranked.

## Tier 1 — do this, it's nearly passive
1. **List on Etsy** (see `etsy-listing.md`). Etsy *is* the traffic. Use all 13
   tags, both keyword-rich title and description. This alone can carry the goal.
2. **Turn on auto-renew** so the listing relists after every sale and stays live.
3. **Ask every buyer for a review** (the quick-start already nudges them). This
   is the single highest-leverage action — Etsy ranks on reviews.

## Tier 2 — a few hours once, keeps paying
4. **Pinterest** is the best free funnel for spreadsheet templates. Create a
   free business account, make 5–10 pins pointing at your listing using the
   `listing-images` (and screenshots of the real file). Pins keep getting found
   for months — it's the most "passive" promotion that exists.
5. **A free Gumroad listing** as a second storefront + the link you paste
   anywhere.

## Tier 3 — optional, higher effort, higher ceiling
6. **Reddit / forums** where freelancers gather (r/freelance, r/digitalnomad,
   indie-hacker communities). Don't spam — answer a "how do you track your
   finances?" thread and mention your template. One good comment can sell for
   months.
7. **A single TikTok/Reel** screen-recording the dashboard updating as you type.
   These templates go viral surprisingly often. One hit = a month of sales.

## What NOT to bother with
- Paid ads before you have reviews (you'll lose money learning).
- Building your own website/store from scratch (slow, needs traffic anyway).
- Endless tweaking of the product. It's good. Ship it and get reviews.

## The minimum viable routine (≈30 min/week, optional)
- Pin 1–2 new Pinterest pins.
- Send a thank-you + review request to recent buyers.
- That's it. Everything else compounds on its own.

## Honest expectation-setting
No one can *guarantee* €150/month — it depends on search ranking and reviews,
which build over weeks. But the math is small (≈14 sales/month at €12) and the
niche has real, constant demand. The biggest risk isn't the product; it's
delisting too early. Give it 8–12 weeks.
